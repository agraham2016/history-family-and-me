History, Family, and Me

Courage, Strength, and Resilience from those who came before!

History is not just a collection of dates and events in textbooks—it is the living story of our ancestors. By exploring their struggles, triumphs, and everyday challenges, we gain profound insights into our own lives and the world we inhabit today. Understanding where we come from empowers us to navigate our present with greater courage, resilience, and purpose.

Strength from Ancestors

Elder Mark A. Bragg, a leader in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and executive director of the Family History Department, emphasizes how connecting with our ancestors builds Christlike resilience. Their stories become part of us, providing a foundation for facing our own challenges.

“Learn, remember, retell their stories so their courage becomes part of your spiritual DNA.”

The Power of Ancestral Connections

Research consistently shows that knowledge of family history enhances personal well-being.

★

Higher Self-Esteem

Studies from Emory University and BYU show that children and young adults who know their family stories have significantly higher self-esteem.

✦

Greater Resilience

Family narratives help moderate stress and build emotional strength, making individuals better equipped to handle adversity.

✿

Improved Well-Being

Reduced anxiety (up to 20% in one study) and a stronger sense of identity and control over one’s life.

Inspiring Words on Ancestral Wisdom

“The more children knew about their family’s history, the stronger their sense of control over their lives, the higher their self-esteem. The most resilient families pass down stories acknowledging both highs and lows and these ‘oscillating’ narratives show children that families go through disasters and triumphs, and ultimately bounce back.”

Dr. Marshall Duke of Emory University found that a child’s knowledge of their family history is the best predictor of their emotional well-being.
